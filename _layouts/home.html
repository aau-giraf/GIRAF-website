The project

Run by student teams, every fall semester since 2011.

GIRAF is a research and teaching project at Aalborg University's Department of Computer Science. Since 2011, student teams have built and rebuilt a small family of communication tools for children with little or no verbal speech, and tested them in special-needs schools. The apps below are in daily use at our partner school. They are also always partway through another rebuild, because that is how the project runs.

The apps

Three tools, one shared backend.

Each app is a Flutter front end over the shared GIRAF Core service. They are at different stages of maturity, noted on each card.

In daily use

Weekplanner

Caregivers build a child's day and week from pictograms. The child sees what is happening and in what order, and ticks off each activity as it is done.

VTA: communication boards

Boards of visual artefacts, each with an image, a label and a sound, shared live between a child and a caregiver. Includes text-to-speech and video calling.

Our main partner is Egebakken Specialskole in Vodskov, with the Center for Autism & ADHD in Aalborg. Features come from classroom observation, staff interviews and usability testing with real children.

How it's designed

Five principles, for the apps and this page.

From TEACCH and PECS practice.

  • 01

    Predictability

    What happens next is always visible.

  • 02

    Visual clarity

    One idea per card, shown in pictures first.

  • 03

    Simplicity

    As few steps as possible.

  • 04

    Consistency

    The same action looks and works the same way everywhere.

  • 05

    Independence

    The child can do it without help.
