## What is GIRAF?

GIRAF (originally "Graphical Interface Resource for Autistic Folks") is a collection of digital tools developed for autistic children with little or no verbal communication.

Our tablet-based applications help structure daily life by digitizing the physical support systems that already work - schedule boards, picture cards, and visual aids.

## Why Visual Tools?

Many autistic individuals benefit from visual supports that provide:

- **Predictability** - Knowing what comes next reduces anxiety
- **Consistency** - Digital tools behave the same way every time
- **Independence** - Children can check their own schedules without adult assistance
- **Portability** - Tablet apps travel easily between home and school

## Our Target Users

GIRAF applications are designed for:

- **Children with autism** (primarily ages 5-12)
- **Children with little or no verbal communication**
- **Educational and care settings** in Denmark

Our designs are informed by direct collaboration with educators and caregivers who work with these children daily.

## Our Partners

We develop in close cooperation with Danish institutions:

- [Egebakken Specialskole](https://egebakken.aula.dk/) - A specialized school for children with autism
- [Center for Autism and ADHD](https://handicaptilbuddene.aalborg.dk/fagcentre/fagcenter-for-autisme-og-adhd) - Aalborg Municipality

These partnerships ensure our applications address real needs and work in real settings.

## Academic Home

GIRAF is developed by students at the **Department of Computer Science, Aalborg University** as part of their software engineering education.

The project has been running continuously since Spring 2011, with new student teams contributing each semester. This model provides students with valuable experience in:

- Working on real assistive technology
- Collaborating with actual end users
- Contributing to a long-running open-source project
- Multi-team coordination and handover

## Open Source

All GIRAF applications are open source and available on [GitHub](https://github.com/aau-giraf).
